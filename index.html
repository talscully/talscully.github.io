Hi! I'm Tal, a PhD student in the Harvard Systems, Synthetic, and Quantitative Biology program, and I do research in Allon Klein's lab.

I am studying the evolution of immune cells in tunicates, the group of invertebrate animals that are most closely related to us. Tunicates lack vertebrate-specific features, including red blood cells and adaptive immune cells like B and T cells. Our research has shown that these animals have a highly complex set of immune cell types, and that their immune cells are actually dramatically different from immune cells in humans. These results demonstrate the plasticity of immune cell types at large evolutionary distances, and suggest that future study of tunicate and invertebrate immune systems could reveal new strategies used by animals to defend against bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens.
